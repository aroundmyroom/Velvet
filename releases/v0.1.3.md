# Velvet v0.1.3 — Sonos queue mirroring

The Sonos app now reflects Velvet's queue, with per-row art in CLIC and
two-way pause/control sync.

## Sonos
- **The queue shows in the Sonos app.** While casting, Velvet mirrors a window of
  the player queue — the current track plus up to ~30 upcoming — onto the Sonos
  queue (`POST /api/v1/sonos/cast-queue`). Playback starts immediately on the
  current track; the rest of the window fills in the background. The web player
  stays the source of truth.
- **Per-row album art in CLIC.** Queue-row art is served through the speaker's own
  `/getaa` proxy, which extracts the embedded cover from the stream. Transcoded
  streams (where the cover is dropped) fall back to the cached `/album-art/` URL.
  CLIC shows title, duration, and per-row art.
- **Two-way pause + cede control.** Pause/resume from the Sonos app or CLIC is now
  reflected back in the web player (and the sleep LED follows). If you navigate on
  the Sonos app (next / previous / shuffle), the web player notices it's no longer
  on our current track and **cedes control** — it pauses and stops syncing until
  you press Play in the web again, which re-casts and takes control back.
- **Never deletes a queue that isn't ours.** When you switch output or close the
  tab, Velvet wipes the Sonos queue only if every track is Velvet's
  (`POST /api/v1/sonos/queue/clear`) — a user-built queue (e.g. Spotify) is left
  untouched.

## Note
The official Sonos S2 app resolves queue-row metadata through a registered music
source rather than the stored track metadata, so its **queue list rows stay blank**
for HTTP-streamed content. This is a structural S2 limitation — the now-playing
view renders title and art fully in every app, and CLIC shows the full queue.
